## Context

Describe the situation that led to this decision.

Include:
- the problem being addressed,
- relevant constraints,
- assumptions that influenced the decision,
- and any environmental or organizational factors that matter.

This section should make it clear **why a decision was necessary**.

---

## Decision

State the decision clearly and unambiguously.

This should be written as a declaration, not a discussion.

Example:
> We will introduce a stable, human-owned execution surface and prohibit agents from inventing execution logic dynamically.

---

## Rationale

Explain *why* this decision was made.

Include:
- alternatives that were considered,
- tradeoffs that were accepted,
- and why this option was preferred.

Focus on reasoning, not implementation detail.

---

## Consequences

Describe the expected effects of this decision.

Include:
- positive outcomes,
- known limitations,
- risks introduced,
- and any follow-on work required.

Consequences should cover both technical and organizational impact.

---

## Scope and Applicability

Define where this decision applies.

Clarify:
- which systems or contexts are affected,
- where the decision does *not* apply,
- and any conditions under which it may be revisited.
